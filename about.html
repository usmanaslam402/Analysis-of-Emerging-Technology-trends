Welcome to techtrendz

A plateform providing Awareness about Emerging Technology Trends around the globe from 2014-18.

techtrendz analyzes the evolution of Emerging Technology Trends around the globe from 2014 to 2018, using as input the predictions made in the Horizon Report, on Higher Education, Horizon Report is published yearly since 2004. Each edition attempts to forecast the most promising technologies likely to impact along three horizons: the short term (the year of the report), the midterm (the following two years), and the long term (the following four years)

Applies Bibliometric Analysis based on Springer, Hindawi, JSTOR, IEEE, Wiley Online Library, Questia & Web of Science (WOS) and Social Interest based on Google Trends (GT) to these predictions in order to discover which technologies were successful and really impacted, and which ones failed to have the predicted impact and why?

Selected Technologies are being taken from these three Platforms:

  • HORIZON REPORT PREDICTIONS
  • MIT TECHNOLOGY REVIEW
  • IEEE PREDICTED TECHNOLOGIES

Social Interest:

Social interest is a capability inherent in all humans but should be developed and skilled. It comes while someone has an inner sense of connectedness and continuity with others and with all of existence and is felt through others. Social interest is exceptionally developed, herbal empathy for all. Social interest is missing greater often than we comprehend. To analyse the social interest, we will use the Google Trends website provided by the Google that analyses the recognition of peak search queries in Google Search throughout various areas and languages. This site makes use of graphs to compare the quest quantity of various queries over the years.

Bibliometric Analysis:

Scientific impact has become synonymous with the counting of citations. Scientific impact is traditionally measured through the use of author metrics such as per-author and per-journal citation countsThis module will analyse the contributions of the researchers in highly ranked journals and conferences in a specific period of time. This module will be heled full in analysing the published work of the researchers in selective databases of Web of science such as Social Citation Index(SCI), Social Science Citation Index(SSCI), Arts and Humanities Citation Index(A&HCI).This module will also analyse the scientific impact of the published work of the researchers on Springer, Hindawi, JSTOR, IEEE, Wiley Online Library, Questia & Web of Science (WOS) as well.


Weighted Average, Aggregade Weighted Average and Accuracy Percentage Formulas were used
